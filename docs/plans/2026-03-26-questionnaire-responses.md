## SECTION 1: THE FOUNDER

**1.1 Pilates journey:**
I have always had an interest in fitness, having been a "gym bunny" in university, but my love faded for that and I started seeing an increased amount of people on social media doing reformer pilates, I really wanted to try it, so two years ago I went to my first session and loved it. Fast forward to now, I've been going twice a week since and I'm in the advanced class. I then thought how can I develop from here? That's when I decided to train to become a teacher, completing my Mat Pilates course November 2025, and recently completed my Reformer exams.

**1.2 Qualifications:**
I now hold two YMCA accredited qualifications, one level 3 Mat Pilates and level 3 Reformer Pilates. I am looking to get my pre and post natal qualifications by the end of next year and the following year my qualifications for GP and physio referrals.

**1.3 Professional background:**
I have a first class degree in Business Management and Marketing.

**1.4 Success in one year:**
Success in the first year would be consistent full classes each week, gaining 'regular' clients who return each week. A strong number of 1 to 1 sessions. Another instructor to take a couple of the classes each week, otherwise I will end up working 6/7 days a week! Successful community marketing strategies from the idea of once a month on a Saturday morning doing a pilates and coffee morning to my idea of doing a once a month pilates and walk club to encourage people to build friendships as well as fitness together.

**1.5 Success in five years:**
In five years I would like a minimum of 2 other studios that I am involved with, a strong community that follows and interacts with the brand. Franchising options?

**1.6 Public face comfort:** 5/10 — "I don't feel that confident in front of the camera, but it is something I can build upon."

**1.7 Gus's role:** He will be an advisor, especially when it comes to growth and thinking big. Continuing to push me.

**1.8 Farm and wellness philosophy:** I truly believe being in the outdoors and being active can be the best medicine. Hence why I would like to use the studio for not just pilates but creating a walk and talk club, potentially using the farm for retreats.
