## 1. Executive Summary

**ALIGN.** is a boutique reformer Pilates studio opening in June 2026 on the high street of Monmouth, South Wales. Founded by Katie Peckham — a YMCA Level 3 qualified Mat and Reformer Pilates instructor with a first-class degree in Business Management and Marketing — the studio will operate as a premium micro-studio with four reformers, offering intimate group classes, one-to-one sessions, and mat Pilates.

**The opportunity is clear.** There is no dedicated boutique reformer Pilates studio in Monmouth or its immediate surroundings. The town's demographics — the highest house prices in Wales, a large professional commuter population, median age 49 — are a near-perfect match for premium Pilates. The global Pilates market is growing at 14.3% CAGR. Franchise chains have not yet reached South Wales. The window is open.

**The strategy is community-first.** ALIGN. will not compete on price or scale. It will compete on intimacy, personalisation, education, and community — building a loyal base that no competitor can replicate. The walk and talk club, the Pilates and coffee mornings, the science-based education content, and the partnership with Coffi Lab next door are not side activities. They are the strategy.

**The financial plan is conservative.** Katie targets £6,000-£7,000 per month in Year 1 revenue on a budget under £20,000, with profitability from Year 1. Our modelling shows this is achievable with 2-3 classes per day at 75%+ occupancy, supplemented by one-to-one sessions and mat classes.

**The growth path is staged.** Year 1: prove the model, build the community, hire a second instructor. Year 1-2: launch farm retreats, deepen clinical partnerships. Year 2-3: open a second location. Year 3+: explore franchising. Digital content builds gradually throughout, respecting Katie's current camera comfort level (5/10) while systematically developing it.

**The competitive moat is community.** PT Pilates may have a loyal client base. Future chains may have bigger budgets. But nobody will match the depth of community connection that Katie is building — because it cannot be bought, only earned.

**The approach is not aggressive — it is strategic.** ALIGN. does not need to take clients from PT Pilates. Monmouth has zero dedicated reformer studios, which means the market is underserved, not oversaturated. Your job is to grow the market, not fight over the existing one.

## 4. The Micro-Studio Advantage

Four reformers is not a limitation. It is a premium positioning.

### 4.1 Why Small Is Powerful

**Forma Pilates** in Los Angeles operates with just 3-4 clients per session. They charge $75-$500 per session, have a referral-only entry policy, and count Hailey Bieber and Kendall Jenner among their clients. They have expanded to New York and Texas — not by getting bigger, but by staying small and exclusive.

**Core & Co** in Nailsworth (Cotswolds, population ~6,000 — smaller than Monmouth) runs a boutique reformer studio where their 4-week Beginner Series consistently sells out. Their affluent rural-professional demographic is nearly identical to Monmouth's.

The micro-studio model works because:

- **Intimacy = Personalisation.** With 4 clients in a room, Katie knows every body, every limitation, every goal. This is impossible at 12+ reformers.
- **Personalisation = Results.** Better instruction produces faster visible results.
- **Results = Retention.** Clients who see results do not leave.
- **Retention = Word-of-mouth.** Loyal clients are the most powerful marketing channel in a town of 10,500.
- **Word-of-mouth = Waitlist.** And a waitlist is the most powerful signal to open a second location.

### 4.2 Revenue Optimisation: Making Every Slot Count

With only 4 reformer spots per class, every empty reformer is lost revenue that cannot be recovered. Here is how to maximise capacity:

**Reformer Revenue Model (Conservative)**

| Metric | Conservative | Target | Stretch |
|--------|-------------|--------|---------|
| Classes per day | 2 | 3 | 4 |
| Days per week | 5 | 6 | 6 |
| Spots per class | 4 | 4 | 4 |
| Occupancy rate | 70% | 80% | 90% |
| Average price per spot | £18 | £19 | £20 |
| **Weekly reformer revenue** | **£504** | **£912** | **£1,152** |
| **Monthly reformer revenue** | **£2,016** | **£3,648** | **£4,608** |

**Additional Revenue Streams**

| Stream | Monthly Estimate |
|--------|-----------------|
| 1-to-1 private sessions (8/month × £50) | £400 |
| Mat classes (4/week × 6 spots × £12 × 70% occupancy) | £806 |
| Intro offers / class packs | £300-£500 |
| **Total additional** | **£1,506-£1,706** |

**Combined Monthly Revenue Projection**

| Scenario | Reformer | Additional | Total |
|----------|----------|------------|-------|
| Conservative | £2,016 | £1,506 | £3,522 |
| Target | £3,648 | £1,706 | £5,354 |
| Stretch | £4,608 | £1,706 | £6,314 |

To hit Katie's £6,000-£7,000/month target, she needs to operate at the stretch scenario for reformers plus additional streams — or, more realistically, ramp up to 3 classes per day at 80%+ occupancy by Month 4-5, while building the 1-to-1 and mat class base.

**Key insight:** The £6,000-£7,000 target is achievable but requires disciplined capacity management. The "First 100 Clients" playbook below is designed specifically to fill those reformer slots from day one.

### 4.3 Recommended Pricing Structure

Based on UK regional benchmarks (reformer classes in South Wales and the South West typically range £15-£25 per session) and Katie's mid-range positioning:

| Offering | Price | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| Single reformer class | £20 | Mid-range for the region |
| 10-class reformer pack | £180 (£18/class) | 10% discount incentivises commitment |
| Monthly unlimited reformer | £99/month | For dedicated clients; caps at ~5 classes/week |
| Monthly 8-class reformer | £75/month | Sweet spot for 2x/week clients |
| Single mat class | £12 | Accessible entry point |
| 10-class mat pack | £100 (£10/class) | |
| Private 1-to-1 (reformer) | £50 | Premium but accessible |
| Private 1-to-1 (duet) | £35 per person | Couples, friends |

**The Intro Offer is critical.** At £39 for 3 classes, it removes the financial risk of trying ALIGN. for the first time. The goal is conversion: 60%+ of intro clients should convert to a pack or membership within their first month.
