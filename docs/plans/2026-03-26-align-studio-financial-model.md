# 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

ALIGN. Studio is a micro-format boutique Pilates studio with 4 reformers and capacity for 6 mat clients, located mid-high-street in Monmouth, South Wales. Katie Peckham will be the sole instructor at launch (June 2026), working 40–50 hours per week across 6 days.

The financial model demonstrates that:

- **The studio is profitable from Month 2** under all three scenarios (conservative, base, optimistic).
- **Katie hits her £6–7K/month revenue target by Month 4–5** in the base case, and by Month 3 in the optimistic case.
- **Year 1 revenue ranges from £48,000 (conservative) to £84,000 (optimistic)**, with the base case at £62,400–£72,000.
- **The setup budget of under £20,000 is tight but achievable**, with a working capital reserve of £1,500–£2,000 built in.
- **A 2nd instructor becomes necessary and affordable by Month 7–9**, unlocking the path to 4+ classes per day and the £8,000–£10,000/month revenue bracket.
- **Farm retreats add £8,400–£14,000 in Year 1** with zero venue cost.
- **A 2nd location becomes financially viable at Month 18–24**, funded from Studio 1 profits.

The key risk is not profitability — it is Katie's personal capacity. As a sole instructor running 2–3 classes per day, 6 days per week, burnout is the primary threat. The 2nd instructor hire is not optional; it is a strategic necessity.

> **Blended average revenue per reformer spot**: Accounting for the mix of memberships, packs, drop-ins, and intro offers, the realistic blended average is **£14–£16 per spot filled**. We use **£15** as the base case assumption.

At a blended average of £15 per reformer spot and £10 per mat spot:

- **Break-even requires ~100 reformer spots + 20 mat spots filled per month**
- That equals approximately **5 reformer classes per week at 50% occupancy + 1 mat class per week at 50% occupancy**
- In practical terms: **2 reformer classes per day, 5 days per week, with just 2 out of 4 spots filled**

> **This is an extraordinarily low break-even point.** It means Katie covers all costs with less than half her capacity utilised. The micro-studio model with low fixed costs is inherently resilient.

## 14.3 Sensitivity: What If Katie Delays the 2nd Instructor?

If Katie teaches all classes herself for the full 12 months:

- **Saves**: £3,600–£5,400 in instructor costs
- **Loses**: The ability to run 4+ classes/day, limiting revenue ceiling to ~£6,500/month
- **Risk**: Burnout. At 3 classes/day, 6 days/week, Katie is teaching 18 classes per week plus admin, marketing, cleaning, and 1-to-1 sessions. This is unsustainable beyond 6–8 months.

> **Recommendation**: Do not delay the 2nd instructor beyond Month 9. The cost is modest (£400–£700/month) and the benefit — both in revenue capacity and Katie's wellbeing — is significant.
