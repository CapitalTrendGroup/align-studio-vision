## 1. Executive Summary

**ALIGN.** is a boutique reformer Pilates studio opening in June 2026 on the high street of Monmouth, South Wales. Founded by Katie Peckham — a YMCA Level 3 qualified Mat and Reformer Pilates instructor with a first-class degree in Business Management and Marketing — the studio will operate as a premium micro-studio with four reformers, offering intimate group classes, one-to-one sessions, and mat Pilates.

**The opportunity is clear and data-backed.** Pilates overtook yoga and strength training as the most popular fitness genre globally in mid-2024, with ClassPass reservations up 62% year-on-year and 27% of all new studio openings now being Pilates — the highest of any modality. Pilates members check in 4% more and cancel 9% less than any other fitness modality. While Monmouth has existing Pilates provision — including PT Pilates, which operates across Usk and Monmouth with a loyal client base, and several mat-based instructors — there is no dedicated, design-led boutique reformer studio with its own branded space, custom app, and community programme. That is the gap ALIGN. fills. The town's demographics — the highest house prices in Wales (£322K-£353K average), the highest gross disposable household income in Wales (£26,030 per head), 30.1% AB social grade households, and a median age of 49 — are a near-perfect match for premium Pilates. Four existing mat Pilates providers confirm demand without serving the reformer group class format.

**The strategy is community-first.** ALIGN. will not compete on price or scale. It will compete on intimacy, personalisation, education, and community — building a loyal base that no competitor can replicate. The walk and talk club, the Pilates and coffee mornings, the science-based education content, and the partnership with Coffi Lab next door are not side activities. They are the strategy.

**The financial plan is conservative.** Katie targets £6,000-£7,000 per month in Year 1 revenue on a budget under £20,000, with profitability from Year 1. Our modelling shows this is achievable with 2-3 classes per day at 75%+ occupancy, supplemented by one-to-one sessions and mat classes.

**The marketing strategy is expert-backed and UK-specific.** Every marketing recommendation is grounded in validated frameworks from practitioners who have built, scaled, and sold fitness businesses — and in real UK data, not assumptions from other markets. Google Business Profile, not Instagram, is the highest-ROI digital channel. Facebook, not TikTok, is where the 45-55 demographic in small-town Wales spends their time. Email has a 37-48% open rate in UK health and fitness — the highest of any industry. And word of mouth, engineered as a system rather than hoped for as a bonus, is the most powerful acquisition channel for any local service business.

**The growth path is staged.** Year 1: prove the model, build the community, hire a second instructor. Year 1-2: launch farm retreats, deepen clinical partnerships. Year 2-3: open a second location. Year 3+: explore franchising. Digital content builds gradually throughout, respecting Katie's current camera comfort level (5/10) while systematically developing it.

**The competitive moat is community.** PT Pilates may have a loyal client base. Future chains may have bigger budgets. But nobody will match the depth of community connection that Katie is building — because it cannot be bought, only earned.

PT Pilates is your biggest competitive reference point. They operate in both Usk and Monmouth, offer reformer classes, and have a loyal client base.

Four reformers is not a limitation. It is a premium positioning.

With 4 clients in a room, Katie knows every body, every limitation, every goal. This is impossible at 12+ reformers. Personalisation produces results. Results produce retention. Retention produces word-of-mouth. Word-of-mouth produces a waitlist. A waitlist is the signal to open a second location.

### 5.2 Pricing Structure

Studio pricing follows the three-tier model validated by UK Pilates consultant Chris Onslow: a premium single-class rate to incentivise commitment, bundle packs with expiry to enforce attendance frequency, and membership for predictable revenue. Fewer visible options reduce decision fatigue and increase conversion.

**Core Pricing (promoted to all clients):**

| Tier | Offering | Price | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Entry** | Intro offer (3 reformer classes) | £39 (£13/class) | Low-barrier first experience. Three sessions is the minimum needed for a client to genuinely integrate Pilates into their routine. Target: 60%+ convert to membership. |
| **Core** | Monthly 8-class membership | £75/month | The recommended starting point. Enforces 2x/week — the frequency needed to see real results. |
| **Premium** | Monthly unlimited membership | £99/month | For dedicated clients attending 3+ times per week. |

**Available but not actively promoted:**

| Offering | Price | When Used |
|---|---|---|
| Single reformer class | £20 | Casual or irregular clients |
| Drop-in (reformer) | £22 | Tourists and visitors |
| Single mat class | £12 | Accessible entry to the brand |
| Private 1-to-1 (reformer) | £50 | Clients seeking personalised attention |
| Private duet | £35/person | Couples or friends |

Additional options such as 10-class packs (£180), mat packs (£100), and annual paid-in-full memberships (£599/year) can be introduced operationally based on demand — but keeping the visible menu simple at launch prevents choice paralysis and steers clients toward the membership model, which generates 20-25% more revenue than pay-per-session.

## 8. The Retention Engine — Keeping Every Client

A 5% improvement in retention produces a 20-25% increase in profit. The difference between 90% and 80% annual retention is the difference between growth and starting over every year.

### 8.1 The Touchpoint System

| Day | Channel | What |
|---|---|---|
| D0 | App push + email | "Welcome to ALIGN. Here is what to expect." |
| D1 | App message (Katie personal) | "Lovely to meet you. How are you feeling?" |
| D7 | App + personal | "How was your first week?" |
| D14 | App push | "Walk & Talk this Saturday — you are welcome." |

| D60 | App + personal | "Two months in. Noticed changes?" + referral ask |

### 8.2 The "Cancel to Reschedule" Policy

No session is cancelled — it is rescheduled. When a client cancels, respond with two specific alternative times. This creates accountability, drives attendance, and prevents the slow drift that precedes cancellation.

### 8.3 Community as Retention Infrastructure

Emma Barry's "Yearn to Return": a feeling so good you are physiologically drawn to come back. Created by: Katie knowing your name, the studio scent, post-class coffee at Coffi Lab, friendships between members, progress visible in the app. Community events are not side activities — they are retention infrastructure.

## 11. Content Strategy — The Minimum Effective Dose

More marketing does not equal better marketing. The smallest, most strategic set of actions outperforms scattered volume.

**Content pillars:** 40% studio life (no camera needed), 25% education, 20% community, 15% founder story.

**One core piece per month** (blog post or video) → fragmented into 8-12 social posts across channels. Sustainable for one person.

**Camera comfort progression:** Months 1-2: no face (studio photos, text overlays). Months 3-4: voice-over Stories. Months 5-6: talking to camera in Stories. Months 7-12: Reels, live Q&As. Authenticity at 5/10 comfort is more powerful than forced confidence.
