# 1. Brand Strategy Overview

## 1.1 Brand Positioning Statement

> **ALIGN. is the intimate, science-led Pilates studio in Monmouth where everybody knows your name — a community committed to wellness, education, and the belief that movement should leave you feeling refreshed, yet challenged.**

This single sentence contains every differentiator you need: intimacy (4 reformers), science (education focus), community (not just a gym), and the emotional promise ("refreshed, yet challenged" — your own words, and they are perfect).

## 1.2 Perceptual Positioning Map — ALIGN. vs Competition

```
                        PREMIUM EXPERIENCE
                              |
                              |
                    ALIGN. ★  |
                              |
        COMMUNITY ————————————+———————————— TRANSACTIONAL
        FOCUSED               |               SERVICE
                              |
               PT Pilates ●   |
                              |
                              |
                        BASIC EXPERIENCE
```

**ALIGN. occupies the upper-left quadrant**: premium experience + community focus. PT Pilates is strong but sits more centrally — they have loyal clients, but they do not have a lifestyle brand, a dedicated app, community events, or the aesthetic identity that ALIGN. brings.

Nobody else in Monmouth occupies your quadrant. That is your space to own.

## 1.3 The "Not Just a Studio" Brand Promise

Every touchpoint should communicate that ALIGN. is three things:

1. **A studio** — Beautiful, intimate, science-based Pilates
2. **A community** — Walk & Talk club, Coffee Mornings, friendships formed through movement
3. **An education platform** — Clients understand *why* Pilates works, not just *how* to do it

When someone asks a client "Where do you do Pilates?", you want the answer to be: "I'm part of ALIGN." — not "I go to a Pilates class." The word "part of" is everything.

## 1.4 Brand Personality in Action

| Touchpoint | How ALIGN. Shows Up |
|---|---|
| Instagram caption | Warm, knowledgeable, never preachy. "Did you know your diaphragm is your body's most underrated muscle?" not "You NEED to try breathwork!" |
| First class greeting | Katie knows your name. She asks about your day. She remembers you mentioned a sore back last week. |
| Studio scent | Fresh, clean, calming — cedar and eucalyptus. Not a gym. Not clinical. |
| Window display | Cream and sage, seasonal foliage, class schedule beautifully designed. Passers-by stop and look. |
| Community event | Free, welcoming, zero sales pressure. The conversion happens naturally because people fall in love with the vibe. |
| App notification | Friendly, not pushy. "Your Wednesday 7am slot is open — fancy joining us?" not "BOOK NOW! LIMITED SPACES!" |

## 1.5 The Intimacy Advantage — "Where Everybody Knows Your Name"

Four reformers is not a limitation. It is your greatest brand asset.

- **Personalisation**: Katie can genuinely know every single client. Their name, their goals, their injuries, their lives.
- **Exclusivity**: Classes feel special because spaces are limited. "Only 4 spots per class" creates natural urgency without any hard selling.
- **Quality**: With only 4 clients, every person gets attention that a 12-reformer studio cannot match.
- **Community**: When you see the same 20-30 faces rotating through classes each week, friendships form. People come for the Pilates; they stay for the people.

Your marketing should lean into this: *"Four reformers. No hiding at the back. Real attention. Real results."*

## 3.2 Content Pillars

| Pillar | % of Content | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Studio Life** | 40% | Renovation progress, equipment arriving, class glimpses, the beautiful space, morning light through the window, reformer close-ups |
| **Education** | 25% | "Did you know?" posts, Pilates science, anatomy tips, myth-busting, benefits explained in simple language |
| **Community** | 20% | Client spotlights (with permission), event photos, Walk & Talk recaps, Coffee Morning snaps, Coffi Lab tags |
| **Founder Story** | 15% | Katie's journey, why she built ALIGN., behind-the-scenes of building a business, authentic moments |

## 3.3 Camera Comfort Progression Plan

This is designed to feel like a supportive friend saying "you've got this" — not pressure to become an influencer overnight.

### Months 1-2: No Face Required

You do not need to show your face to build a powerful Instagram presence. Focus on:

- **Space photos**: The studio in morning light, reformer close-ups, the view from the window, sage and cream tones
- **Flat-lay shots**: Coffee + class schedule, workout gear, branded merchandise
- **Text-over-image**: Educational facts over beautiful backgrounds ("Your spine has 33 vertebrae. Pilates moves every single one.")
- **Boomerangs**: Hands adjusting a reformer spring, feet on the carriage, a candle flickering in Candlelight Pilates
- **Behind-the-scenes**: Time-lapse of renovation, equipment delivery (phone propped up, no face)
- **Client content**: Repost anything clients tag you in

### Months 3-4: Voice First, Then Face

- **Voice-over stories**: Film the studio, your voice explains something. ("So today I want to show you what our Reformer + Breathwork class looks like..." — camera on the reformer, not on you)
- **Q&A text stories**: "Ask me anything about Pilates" — Katie types the answers. Engaging, personal, no camera.
- **Group event photos**: At a Coffee Morning or Walk & Talk, you are one of many faces. Natural, not performative.
- **Teaching clips**: Camera on your hands demonstrating a movement, your voice guiding. Face is secondary.

### Months 5-6: Growing Into It

- **Talking to camera in stories**: Short (15-30 seconds). "Good morning! We've got a full house this morning..." Natural, unscripted.
- **"Day in my life" content**: Quick clips throughout the day, mix of face and space.
- **Teaching tips**: Camera on you demonstrating, face visible but movement is the focus.
- **Event hosting**: Filmed greeting people at a Walk & Talk. Social, not solo.

### Months 7-12: Your Confident Era

- **Instagram Reels with face**: Short, engaging, educational
- **Live Q&As**: "Ask your Pilates questions" — casual, interactive
- **Community interactions on camera**: Like Izzy Samuel's style — warm, personal, knowledgeable
- **Behind-the-scenes of the business**: "What it's really like opening a studio"

The goal is not to become Izzy Samuel by Month 3. The goal is to become a more confident version of Katie by Month 12 — someone who can talk to a camera as naturally as she talks to a client.

# 4. The Izzy Samuel Playbook — Adapted for Katie

## 4.1 What Izzy Built

Izzy Samuel is an Australian Pilates instructor who has built one of the most engaged fitness communities online. Here is what makes her model work:

| Element | Izzy's Approach |
|---|---|

| **Monetisation** | Free YouTube attracts audience, paid website retains them. Clean, simple, scalable. |
| **Voice** | Clear, instructional, warm. Not intimidating. "Options to make things easier or harder." |

## 4.2 What Katie Can Replicate (And What She Should Not)

| Izzy Does This | Katie's Adaptation | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| Daily YouTube content | Weekly Instagram educational posts (no camera needed) | Month 1 |
| Viral challenges | Monthly in-studio "challenges" (e.g., "30 Days of Core" — members track progress in the app) | Month 3 |
| Strong camera presence | Gradual camera confidence plan (see Section 3.3). Start with voice, build to face. | Months 1-12 |
| Online-only community | Hybrid: in-studio community + online engagement. Katie has what Izzy lacks — a physical space where people hug hello. | Month 1 |
| Subscription website | ALIGN. app (already planned) with on-demand content once Katie is filming comfortably | Month 6+ |
| Free YouTube funnel | Free educational Instagram content + free community events (Walk & Talk, Coffee Mornings) as the top-of-funnel | Month 1 |

## 4.3 Katie's Unique Advantages Over Izzy

Izzy built her empire entirely online. Katie has assets Izzy does not:

- **A physical studio**: Real human connection, real community, real hugs at the door
- **The Wye Valley**: A backdrop that makes every photo and video beautiful without trying
- **Coffi Lab next door**: A built-in social hub for post-class community building
- **A rugby club connection**: Content that breaks the "Pilates is for women" barrier — something Izzy has never had
- **A small-town network**: In Monmouth, one delighted client tells 50 people. Online, one follower tells nobody.
- **The farm**: Future retreat potential that creates an in-person experience no online creator can match

Katie does not need to become Izzy. Katie needs to become the *local* version of what Izzy represents: a trusted, knowledgeable, warm fitness professional who builds a community around her, not just a class schedule.

# 6. Competitive Differentiation — ALIGN. vs PT Pilates

Katie is currently a PT Pilates client. This is delicate. The strategy is **differentiation, not aggression**. In a small town, professional respect matters. You are not taking their clients — you are offering something they do not.

## The Differentiation Matrix

| Dimension | PT Pilates | ALIGN. |
|---|---|---|
| **Brand aesthetic** | Functional, established | Design-led, aspirational, Instagram-worthy |
| **Studio experience** | Proven and comfortable | Sensory, intimate, sanctuary-like |
| **Technology** | Standard booking platform | Dedicated ALIGN. app with progress tracking, community, education |
| **Community events** | Classes only | Walk & Talk, Coffee Mornings, Workshops, Retreats (future) |
| **Education** | In-class instruction | Science-based education content — blog, Instagram, workshops, app |
| **Male appeal** | Likely female-dominated | Gender-neutral branding, rugby club content, "not just for women" messaging |
| **Digital presence** | Local reputation, word of mouth | Active Instagram, Google Business Profile, app, content marketing |
| **Class size** | Larger groups | Maximum 4 (unmatched personal attention) |
| **Positioning** | "Pilates studio" | "Wellness community committed to education and movement" |

## How to Talk About It

- **Never mention PT Pilates by name** — ever, to anyone, in any context
- **Focus on what you offer**, not what they lack: "At ALIGN., we believe in the science behind the movement — that is why we educate, not just instruct"
- **Be gracious**: If a potential client says "I go to PT Pilates," respond: "They are brilliant — and the great thing about having two studios in the area is that you can find the style that suits you best. We would love to have you try a class."
- **Compete on experience**: The moment someone walks into ALIGN. and smells cedar, sees the sage and cream palette, hears the curated soundscape, and has Katie greet them by name — the differentiation is felt, not explained

# 10. Year 1 Marketing Budget

Working within the under-£20K total investment, here is a realistic marketing allocation. Marketing does not need to be expensive — it needs to be consistent and strategic.

| Item | Estimated Cost | Priority | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Window graphics/signage** | £300-500 | Essential | "Coming Soon" vinyl, then permanent class schedule + brand display. Huge ROI — thousands of daily foot traffic. |
| **Printed materials** | £150-250 | Essential | 500 flyers (£80), 250 business cards (£30), Coffi Lab table cards (£40). Reprint quarterly. |
| **Photography** | £0-200 | High | Start with smartphone (iPhone/Android in portrait mode with natural light is genuinely excellent). Budget £200 for a one-off professional shoot of the finished studio if desired. |
| **Instagram/Facebook ads** | £100-200/month | Medium | Start Month 2. Target: women and men 25-55, within 10 miles of Monmouth, interested in fitness/wellness/Pilates. Even £100/month reaches thousands locally. |
| **Community event costs** | £30-50/month | High | Coffee Morning coffee (£5 x 6 people = £30), Walk & Talk is free, workshop refreshments (£20). |
| **Google Business Profile** | £0 | Essential | Completely free. Set up this week. Add photos weekly. |
| **Branded merchandise** | £200-400 | Low (Month 3+) | Branded water bottles or tote bags for founding members or giveaways. Wait until brand is established. |
| **QR code materials** | £20-50 | Essential | Printed QR codes for window, door, Coffi Lab, flyers. Nearly free. |
| **Local press** | £0 | High | Monmouthshire Beacon and local community websites will cover a new business opening as news. No cost — just send a press release and a good photo. |
| **Website/landing page** | £0-100 | Medium | If the app serves as the primary booking tool, a simple one-page website (Carrd.co at £19/year) with booking link, about, and photos is sufficient. |

# 11. Metrics & Tracking

What Katie should measure monthly — and what "good" looks like.

| Metric | How to Track | Month 1 Target | Month 3 Target | Month 6 Target |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Active clients** (attended 1+ class in past 30 days) | Booking system / app | 40 | 100 | 130 |
| **Instagram followers** | Instagram insights | 300 | 600 | 1,000 |
| **App downloads** | App store analytics | 50 | 150 | 250 |
| **Google Reviews** (count and average rating) | Google Business Profile | 15 reviews, 5.0 avg | 35 reviews, 4.9 avg | 60 reviews, 4.8+ avg |
| **Class fill rate** | Booking system | 60% | 75% | 85% |
| **"How did you hear about us?"** | Ask every new client (add to intake form) | Track from Day 1 | Review monthly to see which channels work | Double down on top 3 sources |
| **Community event attendance** | Headcount | 8-10 per event | 12-15 per event | 15-20 per event |
| **Referral conversions** | Track referral codes or ask | 2 | 10 | 25 total |
| **Client retention** (% who rebook within 30 days) | Booking system | 60% | 70% | 75% |
| **Revenue** | Accounting | £3,000-4,000 | £5,500-6,500 | £6,000-7,000 |

### The "How Did You Hear About Us?" Question

This is the single most important data point in your first 6 months. Add it to your intake form (in the app or on paper). Options:

- Walked past the studio / saw the window
- Coffi Lab
- Instagram / Facebook
- Google search
- Friend or family referral (who?)
- Community event (Walk & Talk / Coffee Morning)
- Rugby club connection
- Local press / Monmouthshire Beacon
- Other

Review this monthly. If 40% of clients are coming from the shop front window, invest more in window displays. If referrals are your biggest channel, double down on the referral programme. Let the data guide your marketing spend.

# Final Words

Katie, here is the truth about getting clients: you do not need a massive marketing budget, a viral video, or a perfect Instagram feed. You need a beautiful space that people love walking into, a genuine warmth that makes them feel known, and the consistency to show up — on social media, at community events, and at the studio door — week after week.

You have every single one of those things.

The first 20 clients will come from your window and your network. The next 30 will come from those 20 telling their friends. The next 50 will come from your community events, your Google reviews, and the momentum that builds when a small town falls in love with something new.

One hundred clients in three months is not a stretch. In a town with no reformer competition, affluent demographics, and a founder with a first-class marketing degree, it is simply the natural outcome of showing up and doing the work.

You have got this.
